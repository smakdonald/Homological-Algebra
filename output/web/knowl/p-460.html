Similarly, the cokernel of \(f\) fits into an exact sequence
\begin{equation*} A \stackrel{f}{\longrightarrow} B \longrightarrow \operatorname{coker} f \longrightarrow 0 \end{equation*}
which any right exact functor \(G\) will take to an exact sequence
\begin{equation*} G(A) \stackrel{G(f)}{\longrightarrow} G(B) \longrightarrow G(\operatorname{coker} f) \longrightarrow 0 \end{equation*}
Exactness says that \(G(\operatorname{coker} f)\) is the cokernel of \(G(f)\text{.}\)
