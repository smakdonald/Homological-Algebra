Let \(h:\left(F_{\bullet}, \partial_{\bullet}^{F}\right) \longrightarrow\left(G_{\bullet}, \partial_{\bullet}^{G}\right)\) be a map of complexes. For all \(n, h_{n}\) restricts to homomorphisms \(B_{n}(h): B_{n}\left(F_{\bullet}\right) \longrightarrow B_{n}\left(G_{\bullet}\right)\) and \(Z_{n}(h): Z_{n}\left(F_{\bullet}\right) \longrightarrow Z_{n}\left(G_{\bullet}\right)\text{.}\) As a consequence, \(h\) induces homomorphisms on homology \(\mathrm{H}_{n}(h): \mathrm{H}_{n}\left(F_{\bullet}\right) \longrightarrow \mathrm{H}_{n}\left(G_{\bullet}\right)\text{.}\)
