Definition 2.20.

Given any map of complexes \(f: B_{\bullet} \longrightarrow C_{\bullet}\text{,}\) the kernel of \(f\) is the subcomplex \(\ker f\) of \(B_{\bullet}\) that we can assemble from the the kernels \(\ker f_{n}\text{.}\) More precisely, \(\operatorname{ker} f\) is the complex
\begin{equation*} \cdots \longrightarrow \operatorname{ker} f_{n+1} \longrightarrow \operatorname{ker} f_{n} \longrightarrow \operatorname{ker} f_{n-1} \longrightarrow \cdots \end{equation*}
where the differentials are simply the corresponding restrictions of the differentials on \(B_{\bullet}\text{.}\) Similarly, the image of \(f\) is the subcomplex of \(C\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \cdots \longrightarrow \operatorname{im} f_{n+1} \longrightarrow \operatorname{im} f_{n} \longrightarrow \operatorname{im} f_{n-1} \longrightarrow \cdots \end{equation*}
where the differentials are given by restriction of the corresponding differentials in \(C_{\bullet}\text{.}\) The cokernel of \(f\) is the quotient complex \(C_{\bullet} / \operatorname{im} f\text{.}\)
