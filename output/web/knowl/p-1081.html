Let \(f: A \longrightarrow B\) be an injective \(R\)-module homomorphism. There is a commutative diagram
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} & \left(\bigoplus_{i \in I} M_{i}\right) \otimes_{R} A \stackrel{\cong}{\longrightarrow} \bigoplus_{i \in I} M_{i} \otimes_{R} A \\ & \varphi:=1 \otimes f \downarrow \\ & \left(\bigoplus_{i \in I} M_{i}\right) \otimes_{R} B \stackrel{\downarrow}{\cong} \cong \bigoplus_{i \in I} M_{i} \otimes_{R} B \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
where the horizontal maps are the isomorphisms from TheoremÂ 3.55. In particular, \(\varphi\) is injective if and only if \(\psi\) is injective. Moreover, \(\psi\) is injective if and only if each component is injective, meaning \(1 \otimes f: M_{i} \otimes A \longrightarrow M_{i} \otimes B\) is injective for all \(i\text{.}\)
