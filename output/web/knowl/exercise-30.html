Exercise 54. Let \(R\) be a commutative noetherian ring, \(W\) be a multiplicative set, \(M\) be a finitely generated \(R\)-module, and \(N\) an arbitrary \(R\)-module. Show that
\begin{equation*} \operatorname{Hom}_{W^{-1} R}\left(W^{-1} M, W^{-1} N\right) \cong W^{-1} \operatorname{Hom}_{R}(M, N) . \end{equation*}
In particular, if \(P\) is prime,
\begin{equation*} \operatorname{Hom}_{R_{P}}\left(M_{P}, N_{P}\right) \cong \operatorname{Hom}_{R}(M, N)_{P} \end{equation*}
