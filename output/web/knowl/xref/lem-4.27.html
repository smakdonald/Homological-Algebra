Corollary 4.28. Injectives Closed Under Quotients in PIDs.

Over a principal ideal domain, quotients of injective modules are injective.

Proof.

If \(E\) is injective, it is also divisible, by Lemma 4.24. Given any submodule \(D \subseteq E\text{,}\) any \(e \in E\text{,}\) and a nonzero \(r \in R\text{,}\) there exists \(y \in E\) such that \(r y=e\text{,}\) and so this also holds in \(E / D\text{.}\) Then \(E / D\) is divisible, and thus injective by Theorem 4.27.  1
