Theorem 16.17.

Let \(R\) be a Noetherian ring, \(I\) an ideal of \(R\text{,}\) and \(M\) a finitely generated \(R\)-module such that \(IM\neq M\text{.}\) Let \(x_1,\dots,x_r\) be a maximal \(M\)-sequence contained in \(I\text{.}\) Then \(r=\sup\{n\mid \operatorname{Ext}^n_R(R/I,M)\neq 0\}.\) Consequently, all maximal \(M\)-sequences contained in \(I\) have the same length and
\begin{equation*} \operatorname{grade}_IM=\inf\{n\mid \operatorname{Ext}^n_R(R/I,M)\neq 0\}. \end{equation*}
In particular, \(\operatorname{grade}_I M<\infty\text{.}\)

Proof.

Notice that the right-hand-side is \(g:=g(R/I,M)\text{.}\) Suppose \(r=0\text{.}\) Since \(IM\neq M\text{,}\) it must be that \(I\) consists of zero-divisors on \(M\text{.}\) Thus, \(I\) is contained in the union of the associated primes of \(M\) (Grifo 905, Theorem 6.27). By the prime avoidance lemma (Grifo 905, Theorem 3.29), \(I\subseteq p\) for some associated prime \(p\) of \(M\text{.}\) Then the composition \(R/I\to R/p\hookrightarrow M\) is nonzero. Hence, \(\operatorname{Hom}_R(R/I,M)=\operatorname{Ext}^0_R(R/I,M)\neq 0\) and \(g=0=r\text{.}\) Proceeding by induction on \(r\text{,}\) we may assume \(r>0\) and that the result holds for all finitely generated \(R\)-modules \(N\) with \(IN\neq N\) and having a maximal \(N\)-sequence of length at most \(r-1\text{.}\) Let \(N=M/x_1M\text{.}\) Then \(IN\neq N\) and \(x_2,\dots, x_r\) is a maximal \(N\)-sequence contained in \(I\text{.}\) Hence, \(r-1=g(R/I,N)=g-1\) by Proposition PropositionÂ 16.16. Thus, \(r=g\text{,}\) which completes the proof.
