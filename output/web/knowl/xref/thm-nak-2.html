Theorem 11.46. NAK 2.

Let \((R,\fm,k)\) be a local ring, and \(M\) be a finitely generated module. If \(M=\fm M\text{,}\) then \(M=0\text{.}\)

Proof.

By PropositionÂ 11.45, there exists an element \(r\in 1+\fm\) that annihilates \(M\text{.}\) Notice that \(1 \notin \fm\text{,}\) so any such \(r\) must be outside of \(\fm\text{,}\) and thus a unit. Multiplying by its inverse, we conclude that \(1\) annihilates \(M\text{,}\) or equivalently, that \(M=0\text{.}\)
