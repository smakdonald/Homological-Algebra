Corollary 4.11.

Let \(R\) be any ring.
  1. Every direct summand of a projective module is projective.
  2. Every direct sum of projective modules is projective.

Proof.

  1. Suppose \(M \oplus A \cong P\) for some projective module \(P\text{.}\) By Theorem 4.9, there exists a free \(R\)-module \(F\) and an \(R\)-module \(B\) such that \(P \oplus B \cong F\text{.}\) Then \(M \oplus A \oplus B \cong P \oplus B \cong F\text{,}\) and by Theorem 4.9 this implies \(M\) is projective.
  2. Let \(\left\{P_{i}\right\}_{i \in I}\) be a family of projective modules. By Theorem 4.9, there exist free modules \(F_{i}\) such that each \(P_{i}\) is a direct summand of \(F_{i}\text{.}\) Therefore, \(\oplus P_{i}\) is a direct summand of \(\oplus_{i} F_{i}\text{,}\) which is also free. By Theorem 4.9, this implies that \(\oplus P_{i}\) is projective.
