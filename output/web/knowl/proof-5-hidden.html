If \(f:\left(F_{\bullet}, \partial_{\bullet}^{F}\right) \longrightarrow\left(G_{\bullet}, \partial_{\bullet}^{G}\right)\) and \(g:\left(G_{\bullet}, \partial_{\bullet}^{G}\right) \longrightarrow\left(F_{\bullet}, \partial_{\bullet}^{F}\right)\) are such that \(f g\) is homotopic to \(1_{G_{\bullet}}\) and \(g f\) is homotopic to \(1_{F_{\bullet}}\text{,}\) then by Lemma 2.9 the map \(f g\) induces the identity map on homology. So for all \(n\) we have
\begin{equation*} \mathrm{H}_{n}(f) \mathrm{H}_{n}(g)=\mathrm{H}_{n}(f g)=\mathrm{H}_{n}(1)=1. \end{equation*}
Therefore, \(\mathrm{H}_{n}(f)\) and \(\mathrm{H}_{n}(g)\) must both be isomorphisms.
