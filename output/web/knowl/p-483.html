We claim that \(k\) is in fact an \(R\)-module homomorphism. To see that, notice that if \(k\left(m_{1}\right)=a_{1}\) and \(k\left(m_{2}\right)=a_{2}\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} f\left(a_{1}+a_{2}\right)=f\left(a_{1}\right)+f\left(a_{2}\right)=h\left(m_{1}\right)+h\left(m_{2}\right)=h\left(m_{1}+m_{2}\right) \end{equation*}
so that \(k\left(m_{1}+m_{2}\right)=a_{1}+a_{2}=k\left(m_{1}\right)+k\left(m_{2}\right)\text{.}\)
