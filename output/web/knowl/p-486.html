Let \(h \in \operatorname{Hom}_{R}(M, B)\) be in the kernel of \(g_{*}\text{.}\) Then \(g h=g_{*}(h)=0\text{,}\) so for each \(m \in M\text{,}\) \(g h(m)=0\text{.}\) Then \(h(m) \in \operatorname{ker} g=\operatorname{im} f\text{,}\) so there exists \(a \in A\) such that \(f(a)=h(m)\text{.}\) Since \(f\) is injective, this element \(a\) is unique for each \(m \in M\text{.}\) So setting \(k(m):=a\) gives us a well-defined function \(k: M \longrightarrow A\text{.}\)
