Let \(R\) be any ring and consider \(R\)-modules \(A\) and \(\left\{M_{i}\right\}\text{.}\)
  1. For any inverse system \(\left\{M_{i}\right\}\text{,}\) there is a natural isomorphism
    \begin{equation*} \operatorname{Hom}_{R}\left(A, \lim _{i} M_{i}\right) \cong \lim _{i} \operatorname{Hom}_{R}\left(A, M_{i}\right) \end{equation*}
  2. For any direct system \(\left\{M_{i}\right\}\) or \(R\)-modules, there is a natural isomorphism
    \begin{equation*} \operatorname{Hom}_{R}\left(\operatorname{colim}_{i} M_{i}, A\right) \cong \lim _{i} \operatorname{Hom}_{R}\left(M_{i}, B\right) \end{equation*}
Moreover, when \(R\) is commutative, these are isomorphisms of modules.
