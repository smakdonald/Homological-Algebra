The story of the structure of injective modules then continues in a beautiful way. Over a noetherian ring, it turns out that every injective module can be decomposed into a direct sum of injective modules of the form \(E(R / P)\text{,}\) where \(P\) is a prime ideal in \(R\text{.}\) Moreover, the injective modules \(E(R / P)\) are the indecomposable injective modules, so the basic building blocks of injective modules. One can in fact compute the injective hull of any finitely generated \(R\)-module very explicitly.
