Let \(f, g:\left(F_{\bullet}, \partial_{\bullet}^{F}\right) \longrightarrow\left(G_{\bullet}, \partial_{\bullet}^{G}\right)\) be homotopic maps of complexes, and let \(h\) be a homotopy between \(f\) and \(g\text{.}\) We claim that the map of complexes \(f-g\) (defined in the obvious way) sends cycles to boundaries. If \(a \in Z_{n}\left(F_{\bullet}\right)\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} (f-g)_{n}(a)=\partial_{n+1} h_{n}(a)+h_{n-1} \underbrace{\partial_{n}(a)}_{0}=\partial_{n+1}\left(h_{n}(a)\right) \in B_{n}\left(G_{\bullet}\right) \end{equation*}
The map on homology induced by \(f-g\) must then be the 0 map, so \(f\) and \(g\) induce the same map on homology. Here we are implicitly using the fact that \(\mathrm{H}_{n}(f+h)=\mathrm{H}_{n}(f)+\mathrm{H}_{n}(g)\text{,}\) which we leave as an exercise to be further explored in Remark 3.4.
