Example 1.1.20.

  1. The category \(\mathbf{A b}\) of abelian groups is a full subcategory of Grp.
  2. Since every group is a set, and every homomorphism is a function, Grp is a subcategory of Set. However, not every function between two groups is a group homomorphism, so Grp is not a full subcategory of Set.
  3. The category whose objects are all sets and with arrows all bijections is a subcategory of Set that is not full.
