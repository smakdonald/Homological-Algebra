Remark 2.7. Note that saying that if \(f\) is a quasi-isomorphism between \(F\) and \(G\) is a stronger statement that the fact that \(\mathrm{H}_{n}(F) \cong \mathrm{H}_{n}(G)\) for all \(n\text{:}\) it also says that there are isomorphisms \(\mathrm{H}_{n}(F) \cong \mathrm{H}_{n}(G)\) that are all induced by \(f\text{.}\)
