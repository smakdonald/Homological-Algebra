Lemma 2.19. Consider the short exact sequence
\begin{equation*} 0 \longrightarrow A \stackrel{f}{\longrightarrow} B \stackrel{g}{\longrightarrow} C \longrightarrow 0 \end{equation*}
of \(R\)-modules. The following are equivalent:
  1. There exists a homomorphism of \(R\)-modules \(q: B \longrightarrow A\) such that \(q f=\operatorname{id}_{A}\text{.}\)
  2. There exists a homomorphism of \(R\)-modules \(r: C \longrightarrow B\) such that \(g r=\operatorname{id}_{C}\text{.}\)
  3. The short exact sequence splits.
