Example 1.10. Directed Graph Category.

Let \(G\) be a directed graph. We can construct a category from \(G\) as follows: the objects are the vertices of \(G\text{,}\) and the arrows are directed paths in the graph \(G\text{.}\) In this category, composition of arrows corresponds to concatenation of paths. For each object \(A\text{,}\) the identity arrow corresponds to the empty path from \(A\) to \(A\text{.}\)
