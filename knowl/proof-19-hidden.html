Proof. Fix \(c \in C\text{.}\) The map
\begin{equation*} \begin{gathered} A \times B \longrightarrow A \otimes_{R}\left(B \otimes_{R} C\right) \\ (a, b) \longmapsto a \otimes(b \otimes c) \end{gathered} \end{equation*}
is \(R\)-biadditive, so it induces a homomorphism of abelian groups
\begin{equation*} \varphi_{c}: A \otimes_{R} B \longrightarrow A \otimes_{R}\left(B \otimes_{R} C\right) \end{equation*}
This map is in fact a homomorphism of \(R\)-modules when \(R\) is commutative. Moreover,
\begin{equation*} \begin{gathered} \left(A \otimes_{R} B\right) \times C \longrightarrow A \otimes_{R}\left(B \otimes_{R} C\right) \\ (a \otimes b, c) \longmapsto a \otimes(b \otimes c) \end{gathered} \end{equation*}
is also \(R\)-biadditive, and it induces a homomorphism that sends \((a \otimes b) \otimes c\) to \(a \otimes(b \otimes c)\text{.}\) Similarly, we can define a homomorphism
\begin{equation*} \begin{gathered} A \otimes_{R}\left(B \otimes_{R} C\right) \longrightarrow\left(A \otimes_{R} B\right) \otimes_{R} C \\ a \otimes(b \otimes c) \longmapsto(a \otimes b) \otimes c . \end{gathered} \end{equation*}
The composition of these two homomorphisms in either order is the identity on simple tensors, and thus they are both isomorphisms.
