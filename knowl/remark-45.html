Remark A.18. Every free \(R\)-module is isomorphic to a direct sum of copies of \(R\text{.}\) Indeed, letâ€™s construct such an isomorphism for a given free \(R\)-module \(M\text{.}\) Given a basis \(\Gamma=\left\{\gamma_{i}\right\}_{i \in I}\) for \(M\text{,}\) let
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} & \bigoplus_{i \in I} R \stackrel{\pi}{\longrightarrow} M \\ & \left(r_{i}\right)_{i \in I} \longrightarrow \sum_{i} r_{i} \gamma_{i} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
The condition that \(\Gamma\) is a basis for \(M\) can be restated into the statement that \(\pi\) is an isomorphism of \(R\)-modules.
