Since \(R\)-Mod is a locally small category, we saw in Definition 1.34 that there are two Hom-functors from \(R\)-Mod to Set, the covariant functor \(\operatorname{Hom}_{R}(M,-): R\)-Mod \(\longrightarrow\) Set and the contravariant functor \(\operatorname{Hom}_{R}(-, N): R\)-Mod \(\longrightarrow\) Set. In light of ExerciseÂ 3.2, we can upgrade these functors to land in \(\mathbf{A b}\text{,}\) or in \(R\)-Mod when \(R\) is commutative, not just in Set. Note that while there are two Hom-functors, we will sometimes refer to the Hom functor when talking about properties that are common to both of them.
